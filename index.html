The United States' demographic at the highest risk of child abandonment and adult homelessness are lesbian, gay, bisexual, and transgender people. This problem impacts LGBT+ people across all societies, and concerns anyone who would be able to help: that is, anyone who has any contact with an LGBT+ person. LGBT+ people will continue to face denial of support and indifference from public services as long as ignorance of this problem continues.

LGBT+ Homelessness Pictured

An individual facing homelessness any time in their life is likely to experience poor outcomes. The Williams Institute of Law conducted one of few extensive studies on LGBT+ American homelessness. They discovered lesbian, gay, bisexual, and genderqueer respondents were three times as likely to be homeless in the last year than cisgender respondents. Transgender respondents were seven times as likely, and these figures were double looking at only respondents who were black.

Street Kids, a nonprofit researching unaccompanied youth and child abandonment, found that over 4 in 10 surveyed unaccompanied minors identified LGBT - compared to under 2 in 10 who identify in the general population.
